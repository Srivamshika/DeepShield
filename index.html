How to Spot Fake Media

👁️

Visual Glitches & Artifacts

Watch for pixelation, blur, or color shifts around the face edges, especially near the lips and hairline. Deepfakes often struggle with background blending.

👀

Unnatural Eye Movement

Real eyes blink naturally and follow movement smoothly. Deepfakes often have stiff, jerky eye movements, or the eyes don't sync with head movements.

🎙️

Audio & Lip-Sync Mismatch

Watch for lips that don't match the audio perfectly. Even a slight delay or misalignment can indicate synthetic media. Listen for unnatural pauses or rhythm issues.

💨

Inconsistent Lighting

Real lighting matches the environment and remains consistent. Deepfakes often have shadows that don't align with light sources, or lighting that changes unnaturally.

🎵

Voice Artifacts & Synthesis

Listen for robotic tones, unnatural pauses, breathing inconsistencies, or pitch variations. AI-generated voices often lack emotional nuance and natural inflection.

🤖

Limited or Delayed Expressions

Human expressions are natural and immediate. Deepfakes often show limited range, delayed reactions, or expressions that don't match the emotional context of speech.
